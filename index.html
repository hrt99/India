Journey to Freedom: Historical Timeline

1857

First War of Independence

The Sepoy Mutiny marked the beginning of organized resistance against British rule, sparking nationwide awareness about independence and the need for unified action against colonial oppression.

1885

Formation of Indian National Congress

The INC was established as the first organized political platform to voice Indian concerns and demands for self-governance, laying the foundation for the independence movement.

1920

Non-Cooperation Movement

Gandhi launched the first nationwide mass civil disobedience movement against British rule, uniting millions of Indians in peaceful resistance and boycott of British goods and institutions.

1930

Salt March (Dandi March)

Gandhi's historic 240-mile march to make salt became a global symbol of peaceful resistance, breaking the British salt monopoly and inspiring civil rights movements worldwide.

1942

Quit India Movement

Gandhi's final call for immediate British withdrawal mobilized the entire nation in the ultimate push for complete independence, with the slogan 'Do or Die' resonating across India.

1947

Independence Day

India gained freedom at the stroke of midnight on August 15, 1947, marking the birth of the world's largest democracy and the end of nearly 200 years of British colonial rule.
